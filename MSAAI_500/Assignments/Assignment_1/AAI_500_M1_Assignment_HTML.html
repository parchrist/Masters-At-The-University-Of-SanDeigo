Problem # 1.13.¶

A report indicates that public school teachers annual salaries in New York city have an approximate mean of $69,000 and standard deviation of $6,000. If the distribution has approximately a bell shape, report intervals that contain about (a) 68%, (b) 95%, (c) all or nearly all salaries. Would a salary of $100,000 be unusual? Why?

Answer¶

Using the empirical rule, we can say that 68% of the data is within 1 standard deviation of the mean, 95% of the data is within 2 standard deviations of the mean, and 99.7% of the data is within 3 standard deviations of the mean.

With that being said, it is unusual for a teacher to make $100,000. It is within 3 standard deviations of the mean, and 95% of the data is within two. With that being said, this would be considered to be a high salary for a teacher.
